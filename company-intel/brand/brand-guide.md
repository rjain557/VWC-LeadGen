# VisionWise Capital — Brand Guide

*Version 1.0 — extracted from official logo kit (2016, designer Kelly Norman) and reconciled with live site visionwisecapital.com.*

---

## 1. Brand Essence

**VisionWise Capital (VWC)** is a private real estate investment firm based in Southern California that syndicates multifamily and residential deals to accredited investors. Founded and led by **Sanford D. Coggins (SDC)**.

**Brand promise:** *"Quality commercial real estate investments with the security and performance your portfolio deserves."*

**Three traits the brand consistently signals:**
1. **Stewardship** — we treat investor capital like our own.
2. **Discernment** — we say no to most deals so the ones we bring are real.
3. **Relationship** — investors are partners, not transactions.

## 2. Voice & Tone

VWC's voice is **confident, plainspoken, faith-and-values aware, and relationship-first** — modeled on SDC's own writing in the company blogs.

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| Speak like a trusted advisor at a coffee meeting. | Sound like a Wall Street prospectus. |
| Use plain numbers and concrete examples. | Hide behind jargon (alpha, beta, levered IRR-to-equity-multiple). |
| Reference long-term thinking ("decades, not quarters"). | Push urgency or scarcity tactics. |
| Acknowledge faith and values when natural. | Preach. |
| Lead with risk before return. | Lead with return. |
| Use active voice ("We bought, we held, we returned 1.8x."). | Use passive voice or hedging ("It is expected that…"). |

**Tone shifts by audience:**
- **Prospect outreach:** warm, curious, low-pressure.
- **Investor letters / quarterly reports:** candid, specific, accountable.
- **Press releases / press:** factual, third-person, restrained.
- **Pitch decks:** confident, numbers-forward, compliance-aware.

## 3. Logo System

### Primary logo
The horizontal "VisionWise Capital" wordmark with eye/leaf icon. Use for all standard placements: letterhead, decks, web, signage.

- File: [logo.svg](logo/logo.svg) (vector)
- File: [logo-rgb.png](logo/logo-rgb.png) (full size raster)

- File: [logo-rgb.pdf](logo/logo-rgb.pdf) (RGB print, screen)
- File: [logo-cmyk.pdf](logo/logo-cmyk.pdf) (CMYK print, professional press)

### Icon-only mark
Square eye/leaf icon. Use only when the wordmark won't fit (favicon, social avatar, app icon).

- File: [icon.png](logo/icon.png)

### Clear space
Maintain a **margin equal to the height of the "V"** in "VisionWise" on all sides. No text, photos, or graphics inside that margin.

### Minimum size
- Print: 1.0" wide.
- Screen: 120 px wide for the wordmark; 32 px square for the icon.

### Logo dos and don'ts

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| Use the official files unmodified. | Recreate, retype, or "redraw" the logo. |
| Place on white, light gray, or charcoal. | Place on busy photography without a tinted overlay. |
| Use the white/reversed version on charcoal #40464B or darker. | Apply drop shadows, glows, gradients, or 3D effects. |
| Keep proportions locked. | Stretch, squash, or rotate. |
| Use the icon alone only when space forces it. | Use the icon as the primary brand mark. |

### Supporting neutrals (use for layout, dividers, backgrounds)

| Role | Hex | RGB | Usage |
|---|---|---|---|
| White | `#FFFFFF` | 255, 255, 255 | Default background. Required behind the logo unless using reversed version. |
| Off-White | `#F7F7F7` | 247, 247, 247 | Section backgrounds, table row stripes. |
| Light Gray | `#E5E7EA` | 229, 231, 234 | Dividers, borders, table grid lines. |
| Mid Gray | `#8A8F94` | 138, 143, 148 | Captions, metadata, secondary text. |
| Black | `#000000` | 0, 0, 0 | Avoid for body — use charcoal instead. Reserve for legal fine print only. |

### Accent / data-viz colors (charts, callouts)

Derived to maintain accessibility against white and charcoal. Use sparingly.

| Role | Hex | RGB |
|---|---|---|
| Green Dark (text/links on white) | `#5C9627` | 92, 150, 39 |
| Green Tint 20 (chart fills, callout backgrounds) | `#DAF0BC` | 218, 240, 188 |
| Charcoal Tint 80 | `#666B70` | 102, 107, 112 |

### Usage rules
- **Body text** is always Charcoal `#40464B` on white. Never pure black.
- **Links and inline accents** use Green Dark `#5C9627` for AA contrast on white. The brand green `#A3D55D` is too light for body text on white — use it for buttons, dividers, headlines on dark backgrounds, and chart fills only.
- **Section headers** and large display type use Charcoal or Green Dark.
- **No more than two accent colors** on a single layout.

## 5. Typography

### Brand fonts (print, decks, formal documents)

VWC holds licenses for both brand fonts. Templates specify them by name; install the fonts on any authoring machine before generating press-bound output.

| Role | Font | Weights | Source |
|---|---|---|---|
| Headlines | **Gill Sans MT Pro Book** | Book, Bold | Monotype |
| Body | **Proxima Nova Regular** | Regular, Semibold, Bold | Mark Simonson |

### Fallback chain (web, email, machines without brand fonts installed)

When brand fonts aren't available — most often web pages, HTML email, and recipient machines opening DOCX files — fall back to the matched Google Fonts equivalents.

| Role | Stack | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Headlines | `'Gill Sans MT Pro', 'Lato', Helvetica, Arial, sans-serif` | Lato is the closest free Google Fonts equivalent. |
| Body | `'Proxima Nova', 'Open Sans', Helvetica, Arial, sans-serif` | Open Sans is the de-facto Google Fonts substitute for Proxima Nova. |
| Alt headline (web only) | `'Montserrat', Helvetica, Arial, sans-serif` | Used in select web contexts; not a primary brand font. |

The DOCX/PPTX templates name the brand fonts directly. Word/PowerPoint will substitute on machines that lack them; the substitution is acceptable but not equivalent. For documents going to a press, **embed fonts** on save (Word: File → Options → Save → Embed fonts in the file; PowerPoint: same path).

### Type scale (documents, base 11pt body)

| Use | Size | Weight | Tracking |
|---|---|---|---|
| H1 / cover headline | 28–36 pt | Bold | -10 |
| H2 / section header | 18–22 pt | Bold | -5 |
| H3 / sub-section | 14 pt | Semibold | 0 |
| Body | 11 pt | Regular | 0 |
| Caption / metadata | 9 pt | Regular | +10 |
| Disclaimer | 8 pt | Regular | 0 |

### Rules
- One headline font and one body font per layout — don't mix Gill Sans and Lato in the same document.
- Body line height: 1.4–1.5×.
- Never set body smaller than 9 pt.
- Avoid italic body for emphasis — use semibold instead.
- Sentence case for headers; never ALL CAPS except the brand mark.

## 6. Imagery Guidelines

VWC's image library is dominated by **before/after property photography** — this is the visual evidence of the work.

### Property photography
- Daylight, natural color, eye-level.
- Composition shows the full façade or full unit.
- Pair before/after side-by-side at equal size.
- No filters. No HDR. No tilt-shift gimmicks.

### People photography
- Headshots: clean background, neutral expression, business-casual.
- Group/team: candid working shots beat staged "looking at laptop."

### Maps and renderings
- Use only when material to the deal (location, plan, comp set).
- Keep annotations minimal and in brand colors.

### Stock photography
- Avoid generic stock (handshakes, cityscapes, "diverse team smiling").
- If unavoidable, choose images that depict Southern California, multifamily housing, or quiet domestic scenes — never aspirational luxury.

## 8. Compliance & legal boilerplate

Every external artifact mentioning a specific deal, return, or "investment opportunity" must include a Reg D / accredited-investor disclaimer. Pre-written copy is included in the relevant templates. Coordinate with legal before changing it.

**Standard short disclaimer (footer):**
> This communication is provided for informational purposes only and does not constitute an offer to sell or a solicitation of an offer to buy any securities. Any such offer or solicitation will be made only through definitive offering documents (including a private placement memorandum) provided to qualified investors. Past performance is not indicative of future results. Real estate investments involve risk, including loss of principal.
