## How VWC Builds Returns

Across this portfolio, the pattern is consistent:

1. **Sub-institutional target.** 5-50 unit Southern California multifamily — too big for retail buyers, too small for institutional capital. Less competition.
2. **Coastal and infill markets.** Huntington Beach, Laguna Beach, Costa Mesa, Encinitas, Fullerton, San Diego — supply-constrained submarkets with durable rent growth.
3. **Conservative leverage.** Most acquisitions financed at 50% LTV (some Fund IV deals up to 65%). Principal protection over yield maximization.
4. **Value-add lifecycle.** Acquire underperforming asset → renovate exterior + interior → re-tenant at market rents → hold or sell into improved cap-rate environment.
5. **Patience on entry.** The 374 Cypress acquisition shows the pattern: tracked the property for 4 months at $1M below ask before the seller came to terms.
