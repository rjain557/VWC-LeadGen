# 330 W I Street — Encinitas, CA

**Status:** In Renovation | **21 units** | **Acquired:** 9/26/2024

## The Opportunity

VWC's largest acquisition to date — a 21-unit asset in Encinitas, North San Diego County. At $13.15M acquisition price, this deal is roughly 2.3x the size of the next-largest VWC transaction (Costa Mesa, $5.7M). Encinitas is one of the most supply-constrained coastal submarkets in San Diego County — coastal zone restrictions, downtown infill character, and a high-income renter base.

## The Strategy

A heavy reposition: $1.2M projected CapEx — the largest CapEx commitment in VWC's history, at 9.1% of acquisition. The deal is currently mid-renovation. The unit count, total cost, and equity commitment all represent a step-change in VWC's deal size — but the underwriting framework (sub-50% LTV, value-add reposition, coastal submarket) is unchanged.

## Investor Takeaway

The clearest signal of VWC's franchise maturity: a $13M acquisition executed in 2024 with the same 50% LTV discipline that VWC ran on its 2017 $2M Hoskins deal. The framework didn't change as the firm scaled — only the deal size did. For an investor evaluating VWC today, Encinitas is the live demonstration that the operating model holds at scale.
