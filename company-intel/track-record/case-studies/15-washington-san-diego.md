# 630 W. Washington Ave — San Diego, CA

**Status:** Stabilized | **12 units** | **Acquired:** 12/30/2021

## The Opportunity

A 12-unit asset in San Diego — acquired the same day as 9062 Kenwood Dr (Spring Valley) as part of VWC's late-2021 expansion into the San Diego metro. The deal closed in a market peak — the last weeks of 2021, before the rate environment shifted decisively in 2022.

## The Strategy

Standard value-add lifecycle. CapEx of $335K (9.5% of acquisition) covered the planned reposition. The asset is currently held at stabilization rather than sold — VWC has chosen to operate it through the cap-rate widening rather than force a disposition into a weak window.

## Investor Takeaway

This is the only deal in the VWC portfolio currently marked at a paper loss — and it is included in this library because honest track records include the deals that didn't work.

A few things to note:

1. **The loss is on estimated market value, not realized.** The asset is still held and operating. Cap rates compressed dramatically through 2022-2024; San Diego B-class multifamily values have moved meaningfully off the late-2021 entry basis.
2. **50% LTV gave VWC the optionality to hold.** A 70-80% LTV operator would face refinance pressure in this rate environment. VWC can wait.
3. **Portfolio context matters.** Across 23 deals, the portfolio has produced $12.8M in cumulative profit and 23.95% average levered returns. One paper loss in a programmatic strategy is the cost of running enough deals to compound — not a strategy failure.

The story this asset tells is about leverage discipline. Investors who avoided forced losses through 2022-2024 are the ones who set their LTV ceiling before the cycle turned.
